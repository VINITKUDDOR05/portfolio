Street artists

Vinit Kuddor

Street artists - yes, I'm talking about those individuals who showcase their art on the road. For them, the road or path is a stage, and the travelers passing by are their audience. If someone offers them a small compliment or a token of appreciation for their work, they're overjoyed and offer their blessings. In the past, street artists weren't accorded much respect, but today they're considered no less than movie stars. What sets them apart is their unique talent, which makes them special. If you live in big cities like Mumbai or Delhi, you'll likely find a street artist in every lane. Today's story is about these artists, so let's dive in. On May 31, 2024, around 8 pm, I visited Bandstand Seaface in Bandra with my friends. This spot is one of the most popular places in Mumbai, and if you're from the city, you'll know what I mean. It's a hub for Bollywood stars, and Mumbaikars often catch glimpses of them. We arrived there in about 30 minutes, and what caught my eye was a street artist performing acrobatics. Street artists often showcase a range of talents, including dance, singing, playing instruments, yoga, and more. I was particularly impressed by a performer who was doing backflips and handstands. I assumed he was a dancer, but I didn't know his name. I wanted to talk to him, but as an introvert, I hesitated. I wish I had mustered the courage to approach him and learn more about his craft. Perhaps next time I'll try again. As a writer, the more I travel, the more I get to know people closely. However, being an extrovert certainly helps in these situations. There's more to share, like the competition of throwing fists going on nearby, but I'll save that for the next story. Until then, I'll see you in the next installment. .
